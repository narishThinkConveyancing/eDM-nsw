After finding a Buyer, it’s time to “Exchange”. This may include negotiations with the Buyer. When terms are agreed, your Solicitor will prepare two copies of the final Contract so that it can be signed by you and the Buyer.

Copies are then swapped which finalises the Exchange. This newly minted Signed Contract of Sale is considered as a “point of no return” as it firmly binds all parties to the agreement. Note: This process is unique to NSW.

As part of the Exchange Service, you’ll get:

✓   A Solicitor to work with you, Real Estate Agent and the Buyer.
✓   Negotiation/effecting one of the Buyers requests.
✓   Preparation of final documents with all details inserted.
✓   Facilitation of the Exchange process.
✓   Validating the Exchange and providing Signed Contract.

Once complete, Exchange provides you with a Signed Contract to take to Conveyance. We’ll issue the second invoice at this time.

With Exchange completed, the property is off the market, and the Buyer is legally bound. What happens next is conveyancing and settlement. This is the process where payments are collected, and the property is transferred to the Buyer.

As part of the Sellers Conveyancing service, you’ll get:
